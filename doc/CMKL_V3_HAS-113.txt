Details for Competency HAS-113 (AI and Computer Engineering for Community Impact I)

IDENTITY CODE: AI_COMMUNITY_1_4
REQUIRED? false
CREDITS: 4 (Graded)
DESCRIPTION
This competency offers students a unique opportunity to apply their knowledge of AI and computer engineering in real-world community settings. Throughout the time, students will engage in hands-on projects and community service activities designed to address specific needs within local communities. The course emphasizes experiential learning, fostering practical skills, social responsibility, and a deeper understanding of the societal impacts of technology.
Term: Spring (during Winter Break)
Location: Varied (local community sites)
Format: On-site, activities-based, no lecture
RESPONSIBLE INSTRUCTOR: Apirujee Rujirek
PREREQUISITES
None
DISTRIBUTION AREAS
   -- Humanities and Social Sciences

SKILL CODE: HAS-113:00010   
	KNOWLEDGE TOPIC: Community Engagement and Applied AI for Social Impact       
	ASSESSMENT TYPE: Self-assessment     
	ASSESSMENT TITLE: Apply AI and computer engineering concepts to real-world community problems.
	ASSESSMENT DESCRIPTION: Attendance (50%): Active participation and engagement in all on-site activities are crucial. Students are expected to be present, punctual, and fully involved in their assigned projects.
Individual Essay (50%): Each student must submit a reflective essay (500-1000 words) that discusses their experiences, the impact of their work on the community, and personal growth. The essay should include specific examples and insights gained from the course activities.
